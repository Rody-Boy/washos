WHAT IS THIS?
─────────────
This is an animated digital presentation for the Samsung Wash OS product launch.
It plays automatically on the LED wall — like a high-quality video, but built
with web technology so it looks razor-sharp on the LED panels.

There are TWO versions:

   MORNING   →  With voiceover narration (a voice explains each slide)
   AFTERNOON →  Without voiceover (visuals only, for live speaker or background)


================================================================================
HOW TO PREVIEW ON YOUR COMPUTER
================================================================================

You don't need any special software. Just a web browser (Chrome recommended).

  STEP 1: Find the files
  ──────────────────────
  You received a folder called "washos" containing two sub-folders:

     led-wall-resolume/           ← Morning version (with voice)
     led-wall-resolume-no-vo/     ← Afternoon version (no voice)

  STEP 2: Open in browser
  ────────────────────────
  Double-click the file:

     Morning:   led-wall-resolume/samsung_washos_keynote.html
     Afternoon: led-wall-resolume-no-vo/samsung_washos_keynote.html

  It will open in your default web browser.

  STEP 3: Start the presentation
  ───────────────────────────────
  MORNING VERSION:
  - You'll see a dark screen with "SAMSUNG WASH OS" and
    "Click anywhere to start"
  - Click anywhere
  - The presentation begins automatically with voice narration
  - It runs for 4 minutes and 8 seconds, then loops

  AFTERNOON VERSION:
  - Opens and starts playing immediately — no click needed
  - Runs for 4 minutes and 23 seconds, then loops
  - No sound (designed for a live speaker or ambient display)


  NOTE: On your computer the aspect ratio will look different from the
  actual LED wall (your screen is wider/taller). This is normal.
  On the LED wall (4224x896 pixels) it will look perfect.

LED WALL SETUP (WHAT TO TELL THE TECH TEAM)
================================================================================

If you need to brief the tech team, here's what they need to know:

  1. The LED wall resolution is 4224 x 896 pixels (three panels)
  2. The presentation is an HTML file played through Resolume Arena
  3. There's a detailed TECHNICAL_SETUP_GUIDE.txt in the same folder
  4. The folder must be copied to the Resolume machine's local drive
  5. No internet is required — everything works offline
  6. Morning version needs one click to start (for audio to work)
  7. Afternoon version auto-starts — no interaction needed
  8. There's a debug mode (press G) to check panel alignment

  Give the tech team:
  - The "washos" folder (contains both versions)
  - The TECHNICAL_SETUP_GUIDE.txt file
  - This PRESENTATION_GUIDE.txt file

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

  Q: Can the presenter control the slides manually?
  A: Yes. Press the right arrow key to go forward, left arrow to go back.
     The presentation will continue auto-advancing after a manual skip.
     The voiceover for each slide plays regardless of how you got there.

  Q: Can we pause the presentation?
  A: Yes. There's a small pause button in the bottom-right corner.
     Click it to pause, click again to resume.

  Q: What happens after the last slide?
  A: It loops back to slide 1 and starts over.

  Q: Can we skip the voiceover on the morning version?
  A: You can advance slides manually with arrow keys, but the voiceover
     for each slide will still play. To run without voice entirely,
     use the afternoon version instead.

  Q: Does it need internet?
  A: No. Everything is embedded in the files — fonts, images, audio.
     It works completely offline.

  Q: Can we edit the text or timing?
  A: The presentation is built in HTML/CSS/JS. A developer can modify
     it, but it's not recommended to change anything on event day.

  Q: What if something goes wrong?
  A: The tech team can refresh the browser source in Resolume to
     restart. See TECHNICAL_SETUP_GUIDE.txt for detailed troubleshooting.

  Q: Can I preview on my phone?
  A: Yes. Open the GitHub Pages link in Chrome on your phone.
     The layout will look different (your phone isn't 4224x896)
     but you can verify the content and voiceover.

  Q: Is there a video file backup?
  A: The tech team can export an MP4 video as a backup.
     See TECHNICAL_SETUP_GUIDE.txt section 12 for instructions.
